    Guillaume Gautier

    Ph.D. in Machine Learning

    Grenoble-Lille, France Contact GitHub Scholar

    I am currently a postdoctoral researcher at GIPSA-lab in Grenoble, working in the GAIA team with Simon Barthelmé, Nicolas Tremblay and Pierre-Olivier Amblard. For now, I am focusing on understanding the properties of a pretty new and general purpose sampling mechanism called Partial Rejection Sampling. It applies to spatial point processes, distributions on graphs, and many other structures where constraints between variables are involved.

    From 2017 to 2020, I did my Ph.D. at CRIStAL in Lille, where I worked in the SigMA and SequeL teams, under the supervision of Rémi Bardenet and Michal Valko. This thesis was focused on designing sampling methods for Determinantal Point Processes (DPPs), which offer an elegant way to encode the notion of repulsion between points or diversity between items. DPPs are used to select diverse subsets of items or features in your data, to build Monte Carlo estimators with fast rates of convergence, and they also characterize the eigenvalue distribution of some random matrix models.

    Research interests

    Computational statistics, Monte Carlo methods, point processes, simulation algorithms. Interaction between optimization and simulation, e.g., randomized optimization problems.

    Besides, I am the main developer of the DPPy Python toolbox. Take a look at the documentation to know more and play with both discrete and continuous Determinantal Point Processes (DPPs)!
